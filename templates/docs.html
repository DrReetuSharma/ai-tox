üî¨ Explanation of Toxicity Types

Here is a brief overview of the different toxicity types predicted by the app:

  • NR-AR (Nuclear Receptor Androgen Receptor): Measures the ability of a compound to interact with the androgen receptor, potentially disrupting male hormonal balance.
  • NR-AR-LBD (Nuclear Receptor Androgen Receptor Ligand Binding Domain): Focuses on how a compound interacts with the ligand-binding domain of the androgen receptor, influencing male reproductive systems.
  • NR-AhR (Nuclear Receptor Aryl Hydrocarbon Receptor): Indicates the potential of a compound to affect immune response and increase cancer risk.
  • NR-Aromatase (Aromatase Enzyme): Evaluates the impact of a compound on the aromatase enzyme, which regulates estrogen levels, important for both male and female reproductive health.
  • NR-ER (Nuclear Receptor Estrogen Receptor): Assesses whether a compound can bind to estrogen receptors, potentially affecting female reproductive health and increasing cancer risk.
  • NR-ER-LBD (Nuclear Receptor Estrogen Receptor Ligand Binding Domain): Focuses on how compounds interact with the ligand-binding domain of estrogen receptors.
  • NR-PPAR-gamma (Peroxisome Proliferator-Activated Receptor Gamma): Measures a compound's effect on metabolic regulation and fat storage.
  • SR-ARE (Stress Response-Antioxidant Response Element): Evaluates a compound‚Äôs effect on cellular stress response and antioxidant pathways.
  • SR-ATAD5 (Stress Response-ATAD5): Examines how a compound may influence DNA repair mechanisms and stress response related to the ATAD5 protein.
  • SR-HSE (Heat Shock Element): Measures the impact of a compound on heat shock proteins, which help cells respond to stress.
  • SR-MMP (Stress Response-Mitochondrial Membrane Potential): Assesses a compound‚Äôs effect on mitochondrial function and cellular energy production.
  • SR-p53 (Stress Response-p53): Focuses on the compound‚Äôs potential to impact p53, a crucial protein for controlling cell cycle and tumor suppression.
